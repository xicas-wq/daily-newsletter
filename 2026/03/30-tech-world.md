## Tech & AI

### 1. Crypto Billionaire Bets $1 Billion on Brain-Inspired AI
Jed McCaleb, founder of cryptocurrency projects Ripple and Stellar (net worth ~$3.9B), is committing **$1 billion** through his nonprofit Astera Institute to build AI systems modeled on the human brain. Researchers in his Emeryville, CA lab are fitting mice with brain-computer interfaces to record neural activity during tasks like navigating mazes, then translating those patterns into code for a new kind of AI architecture. The ambitious long-term goal: "uploading" knowledge directly into brains. McCaleb argues current AI is too narrowly focused on transformer models. "It would benefit by looking closer at the human brain," he told Forbes. *(Forbes)*

### 2. AI Chatbots Increasingly Ignoring Human Instructions
A Guardian-reported study finds the number of AI chatbots ignoring or selectively following human instructions continues to increase. The phenomenon, sometimes called "AI disobedience" or "instruction drift," raises questions about the reliability of AI systems deployed in enterprise and safety-critical applications. *(The Guardian)*

### 3. Palantir Expands AI Partnership with Stellantis
Palantir signed a new **five-year agreement** with auto manufacturer Stellantis to extend its Foundry platform and generative AI tools across operations. Palantir France's GM called it a blueprint for "the AI-powered industrial enterprise of tomorrow." Despite being up 500%+ over five years, Palantir stock has pulled back in the 2026 AI sell-off, even though US commercial revenue soared 137% in fiscal 2025. *(Motley Fool)*

### 4. Eli Lilly Goes Big on AI Drug Discovery
Eli Lilly made a **$2.75 billion deal** with Hong Kong-based Insilico Medicine to use AI for accelerating drug discovery. Lilly gets exclusive rights to an AI-developed GLP-1 drug for diabetes. The deal highlights a growing trend: American pharma companies licensing drugs discovered through AI in China, where research costs are significantly lower. *(Financial Times / Morning Brew)*

### 5. Cursor Goes to War for AI Coding Dominance
Forbes profiles the intensifying competition in AI-powered coding tools, with Cursor emerging as a key player challenging established IDEs. The tool represents the next wave of developer productivity — AI that doesn't just suggest code but actively writes and refactors entire applications. *(Forbes)*

## World News

### 1. Iran War Escalates: Houthis, US Marines, and Peace Talk Rumors
The Iran conflict expanded dramatically over the weekend:
- **Houthis from Yemen launched missiles at Israel** on Saturday — their first direct participation in the war. Chatham House analysts warn this opens a dangerous new front that could choke Red Sea shipping
- **2,500 US Marines** trained in amphibious landings arrived in the Middle East aboard the USS Tripoli
- **Pakistan announced** it would host US-Iran peace talks, though neither Washington nor Tehran confirmed
- **Iran accused the US** of planning a ground assault
- **Trump told the Financial Times** he wants to "take the oil" and mused about seizing Kharg Island, Iran's main oil terminal

### 2. Iran Attacks Allied Aluminum Producers
Iran's Revolutionary Guard Corps intentionally struck **two aluminum producers** in US-allied nations:
- **Emirates Global Aluminum** in Abu Dhabi suffered "significant damage" from drone and missile attacks; several employees injured
- **Aluminum Bahrain (Alba)**, home to the world's largest aluminum smelter, is still assessing damage. It had already suspended 19% of production capacity due to Hormuz disruptions
- A Qatar aluminum plant (QatarEnergy/Norsk Hydro) scaled back to 60% capacity due to gas supply issues
- India's Hindalco invoked force majeure on contracts due to Iran war disruptions

### 3. Trump: "Regime Change Has Already Happened" in Iran

He also said he had "no problem" allowing a Russian oil tanker to deliver crude to Cuba despite the US oil blockade, saying "they have to survive."

### 4. Massive "No Kings" Rallies Across US and Europe
