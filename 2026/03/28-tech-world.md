## Tech Headlines

### 1. Anthropic Data Leak Reveals Secret "Mythos" AI Model

Fortune reported that a data leak at Anthropic has revealed details of a powerful, secret AI model codenamed **"Mythos"**. The leak sent ripples through the AI industry and rattled cybersecurity stocks. Meanwhile, Anthropic's Claude service experienced a major outage lasting nearly five hours, disrupting users globally.

**Why it matters:** Anthropic has been at the center of controversy since the Trump administration's ban and the Pentagon's order to remove Claude from key military systems. This leak adds fuel to the fire and raises questions about AI security practices at the frontier labs.

### 2. Google vs. Microsoft: The Battle of AI Business Models

The Economist's Schumpeter column examined how **Google's vertically integrated AI approach is beginning to pay off** versus Microsoft's partnership-heavy model. Google controls its own chips (TPUs), models (Gemini), and distribution (Search, Android) — giving it a cost advantage that's becoming increasingly visible as AI spending scales up.

### 3. The War Against PDFs Is Heating Up

In a fascinating Economist piece, startup Factify is trying to kill the PDF — arguing the 30-year-old format is hopelessly incompatible with AI agents and is a common vehicle for cyberattacks. The PDF is the second most common file type online and even has a fan club (the PDF Association), but the AI revolution may finally be its undoing.

### 4. Google's TurboQuant — Extreme AI Compression

Google Research published details on **TurboQuant**, a new approach to AI model compression that dramatically reduces model size while maintaining performance. This could make AI inference significantly cheaper and faster, potentially democratizing access to powerful models on edge devices.

### 5. Shield AI Raises $2B at $12.7B Valuation

Defense AI company **Shield AI** raised $2 billion in funding and acquired simulation company Aechelon Technology. The $12.7B valuation reflects the surging demand for AI in defense, particularly autonomous drone and unmanned systems technology in the context of the Iran war.

### 6. Meta's AI Growing Pains

Meta continues to struggle with its AI ambitions:
- Delayed the rollout of its new "Avocado" AI model due to performance concerns
- Planning sweeping layoffs (potentially 20% of workforce) as AI infrastructure costs mount
- Mark Zuckerberg is reorganizing teams and creating a new "Applied AI Engineering" company within Meta
