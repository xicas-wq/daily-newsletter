## Tech & AI News

### AI Doctor Startup Certuma Raises Funds, Plans FDA Meeting
Serial entrepreneur Martin Varsavsky's company Certuma wants to build the first FDA-approved AI doctor. The startup, which launched this winter, focuses on common conditions like UTIs and sore throats. It plans to meet with the FDA about regulatory approval. The idea raises major concerns about misdiagnosis risks, but with the US facing a physician shortage, AI-assisted diagnostics is gaining traction. Japan is also turning to AI to ease doctor shortages for cancer screenings.

### Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang: "We've Achieved AGI"
Nvidia's Jensen Huang said in a podcast appearance Monday that he believes artificial general intelligence has been achieved. While the statement likely concerns the broader AI industry rather than Nvidia specifically, he noted it could be "possible" for a company to be run by AI. Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak offered a contrasting view: "I don't use AI much" --- expressing skepticism that AI can truly replace humans.

### Alibaba Unveils XuanTie C950 RISC-V Chip for Agentic AI
Alibaba released what it claims is the "highest performing RISC-V CPU in the world," the XuanTie C950, designed to power agentic AI workloads. The chip targets edge computing and AI inference, highlighting China's push to develop alternatives to Western chip architectures.

### LiteLLM Python Package Hit by Supply Chain Attack
The popular LiteLLM Python package was compromised in a supply chain attack, affecting developers who use it as a proxy for multiple LLM APIs. This highlights ongoing security risks in the AI/ML software ecosystem.

### Trump's Anthropic Ban Called "Lawless"
Tech Policy Press reports that the Trump administration's designation of Anthropic (maker of Claude) as a "supply chain risk" and its effective ban from government contracts is being challenged as legally questionable. A US Commission also warned that China's use of open-source AI threatens the US lead in AI development.

### Meta Signs $27B AI Deal
Meta signed a $27 billion artificial intelligence deal, with Nebius (a Yandex spinoff) reportedly winning the contract for AI infrastructure services.

### Microsoft's Windows 11 Fix Criticized
Top HN story (497 points): Microsoft's latest attempt to fix Windows 11 user complaints described as "Flowers After the Beating" --- cosmetic improvements that don't address fundamental user frustrations.
