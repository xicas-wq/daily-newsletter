## Concept of the Day: Oil Shocks & Stagflation

**What is stagflation?** It's the dreaded combination of **stag**nant economic growth + in**flation** — prices rising while the economy slows down. It's the worst of both worlds.

**Why it matters today:** The Iran war is creating a classic oil shock scenario. When energy prices spike:
1. **Costs rise** for businesses (shipping, manufacturing, heating) → they raise prices → inflation
2. **Consumers spend less** on everything else because gas/food eat their budget → economy slows
3. The Fed is stuck: raising rates fights inflation but hurts the already-slowing economy; cutting rates helps growth but fuels inflation

**The 1970s parallel:** The IEA head explicitly compared today's crisis to the 1970s oil shocks (1973 Arab oil embargo + 1979 Iranian Revolution). Those events caused years of stagflation — high unemployment AND high inflation simultaneously. Today's crisis may be even worse because:
- Oil was ~$55-60 expected; it's now near $100-109
- The U.S. is both a major producer AND consumer (unlike the 1970s)
- Global supply chains are more interconnected
- The government shutdown is adding domestic economic drag

**The key question for markets:** Can diplomacy bring oil back to manageable levels before stagflation takes hold? Today's 900-point Dow futures surge shows how desperately markets are hoping for a diplomatic resolution.
