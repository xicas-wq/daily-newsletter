### Tech & AI

#### 1. Axios Package Compromised on NPM -- Malicious Versions Drop RAT
The popular JavaScript HTTP library **Axios was compromised on NPM**, with malicious versions deploying a remote access trojan (RAT). This is the #1 story on Hacker News right now. If you use Axios in your projects, immediately check your package-lock.json for affected versions and update. Supply chain attacks on npm packages continue to be a major threat to the JavaScript ecosystem.

#### 2. Microsoft Unveils "Critique" -- Multi-Model AI System
Microsoft launched a new **deep research system called Critique** that brings multiple AI models together to produce and refine responses. Unlike single-model approaches where the AI sounds confident but may be wrong, Critique "requires every key claim to be anchored to reliable sources." It will be available through Copilot and is aimed at giving Microsoft an enterprise edge over competitors.

In related AI news: **Ollama now supports MLX on Apple Silicon** (in preview), meaning faster local AI inference on Macs. Google also released a **200M-parameter time-series foundation model** with 16K context length.

#### 3. Claude Code Source Leaked via NPM Map File
In a notable security lapse, **Anthropic's Claude Code source code was leaked** through a map file left in their NPM registry package. The story is trending on Hacker News, highlighting the ongoing challenge of properly securing published packages and the risks of including source maps in production builds.

#### 4. GitHub Kills Copilot PR Ads After Backlash
**GitHub backed down and removed Copilot pull request advertisements** after widespread developer backlash. The ads had been showing up in the pull request workflow, frustrating developers who felt the commercial promotion was intrusive in their core development tools.

#### 5. 80% of Americans Use AI, But Want Humans for Finance
A TD Bank survey found **nearly 80% of Americans use AI tools**, but most still want humans making their financial decisions. The survey highlights the gap between AI adoption for everyday tasks and trust in AI for high-stakes decisions involving money.

#### 6. "Fedware" -- Government Apps Spying on Users
A trending Hacker News investigation into **"Fedware"** -- government-mandated or government-built apps that reportedly spy on users harder than the commercial apps they seek to ban. The piece examines surveillance capabilities built into official government applications, raising civil liberties concerns.

### World News

#### 7. Iran War: Day 32 of US-Israel Attacks
The **US-Iran war enters its 32nd day** with mixed signals:
- **Trump told aides** he's willing to end the war even without reopening the Strait of Hormuz (WSJ)
- But he also **threatened to "obliterate"** Iran's energy infrastructure on Truth Social
- Pakistan announced it would **host US-Iran peace talks**, though neither side confirmed
- US gasoline prices are surging as the conflict disrupts global oil flows
- Oil hit $102.88/barrel, highest since July 2022

The contradiction between Trump's private signals (peace) and public statements (escalation) continues to whipsaw markets and create uncertainty.

#### 8. Air Canada CEO Forced Out Over Language Controversy
**Air Canada CEO Michael Rousseau will depart** after offering condolences almost entirely in English following a crash at LaGuardia that killed two pilots -- one of whom was a French-speaking Quebecois. Despite Canada being officially bilingual and Air Canada being headquartered in Quebec, Rousseau doesn't speak French. The Office of the Commissioner of Official Languages received **1,800 complaints**. PM Mark Carney publicly criticized his "lack of compassion and judgment."

#### 9. Netflix Pushes Deeper into Live Sports
Netflix is negotiating to **add more NFL games** to its lineup, potentially including a quadrupleheader on holidays. Currently paying $75M per game in a three-year deal, Netflix sees live sports as key to subscriber growth. This comes a week after screening its first MLB game ($50M/year deal). The streaming giant continues its transformation from on-demand content to a hybrid live/recorded platform.

#### 10. $10 Million Art Heist in Under 3 Minutes
Thieves stole **three paintings worth $10 million** from the Magnani Rocca Foundation near Parma, Italy in less than three minutes. The stolen works: "Fish" by Renoir, "Still Life with Cherries" by Cezanne, and "Odalisque on the Terrace" by Matisse. Authorities say it was the work of a "structured and organized" gang who would have taken more if an alarm hadn't triggered.

#### 11. Hong Kong's Hang Seng Books Worst Month Since Jan 2024
Despite China PMI beating expectations, the **Hang Seng index recorded its worst month since January 2024**. The Shanghai Stock Exchange also sealed its worst month since 2022. The Iran conflict's ripple effects, combined with ongoing concerns about China's economic recovery, weighed on Asian markets through March.
