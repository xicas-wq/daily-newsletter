## Top Stories

### 1. Oil on Track for Record Monthly Surge

The Guardian reports that oil prices are on track for their **biggest monthly gain on record** as the Iran war continues to disrupt markets. The conflict has kept the Strait of Hormuz — through which ~20% of the world's oil flows — in a state of uncertainty. Gold prices remain elevated as investors seek safe havens.

**Why it matters:** Higher oil prices feed directly into higher gas prices, higher inflation, and a tighter Fed. This cascading effect has dominated markets all month and shows no signs of easing.

### 2. Goldman Raises Recession Odds to 30%

Goldman Sachs has raised the probability of a US recession to **30%**, citing higher inflation and a lower GDP outlook as oil prices surge. This is up from their previous estimate and reflects growing pessimism about the economic outlook.

**Why it matters:** When Goldman moves its recession probability, Wall Street pays attention. A 30% chance means Goldman thinks there's roughly a 1-in-3 chance we're heading into a downturn — and the main culprit is the oil price shock from the Iran war.

### 3. Fed's Next Move Could Be a Rate HIKE

CNBC reports that markets increasingly see the Fed's next move as a **potential rate hike**, not a cut. Fed officials warn that the Iran war threatens both inflation and the job market. Investopedia notes multiple Fed officials are sounding hawkish alarms.

**Why it matters:** The entire narrative has flipped. At the start of 2026, markets expected multiple rate cuts. Now, a zero-cut year looks increasingly likely, and a hike is on the table. This is terrible news for borrowers, homebuyers, and stock valuations.

## Concept of the Day: Record Monthly Surge (in Oil)

When we say oil is on track for a "record monthly surge," we mean the **percentage increase** in oil prices during March 2026 is on pace to be the largest single-month gain ever recorded.

**How is this measured?** Oil prices are typically tracked using two benchmarks:
- **Brent Crude** (international benchmark)
- **WTI (West Texas Intermediate)** (US benchmark)

The percentage change from the first trading day of the month to the last gives us the monthly change.

**Historical context:** Previous record monthly surges happened during:
1. The 1990 Gulf War (Iraq invaded Kuwait)
2. The 2008 oil price spike (pre-financial crisis)
3. The 2022 Russia-Ukraine war (brief spike)

**Why this one is different:** Unlike previous spikes that often reversed quickly, the Iran war creates ongoing uncertainty because of the **Strait of Hormuz factor** — Iran has the ability to disrupt a critical global shipping chokepoint. As long as the war continues, the threat to oil supply remains.

**Connection to today's news:** The record oil surge is the root cause of almost every other financial headline right now — from Goldman's recession warning to the Fed's hawkish shift to consumer anxiety. Understanding oil's role as the common thread helps make sense of why "everything feels connected."
