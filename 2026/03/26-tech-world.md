## Tech & AI News

### 1. OpenAI Shuts Down Sora; Musk Doubles Down on Grok Imagine

OpenAI suddenly shuttered its Sora video generation tool. In response, Elon Musk announced he would double down on **Grok Imagine**, xAI's image and video generator — including its controversial "spicy" mode that allows sexually explicit content. The tool has already drawn mounting lawsuits and significant backlash.

### 2. Is Big Tech Facing a "Big Tobacco Moment"?

The New York Times ran a major analysis asking whether social media companies are at the same crossroads the tobacco industry faced in the 1990s. After a California jury found Meta and YouTube **liable for addictive platform design** (not just content), the comparison is getting harder to dismiss. Thousands of similar lawsuits are in the pipeline.

### 3. Arm Holdings Makes Historic Pivot to Chip Manufacturing

Arm Holdings stock surged 16% after CEO Rene Haas announced the company will **manufacture its own AI chips** — a radical departure from its licensing-only business model. Citi called it "the most significant shift in the company's history." The in-house chip is projected to generate **$15 billion in annual revenue**.

### 4. Alphabet's TurboQuant: 6x Less Memory for LLMs

Google's parent company confirmed a breakthrough technology that cuts the memory required to run large language models by **at least a factor of six**. This could dramatically lower AI training costs but sent memory/storage stocks (Micron, SanDisk) tumbling 3%+. The long-term paradox: cheaper AI could actually *increase* total demand.

### 5. Trump's New AI Advisory Panel

President Trump tapped **Mark Zuckerberg (Meta), Jensen Huang (Nvidia), and Larry Ellison (Oracle)** for a new White House AI advisory panel. The administration also issued a legislative framework for a uniform national AI policy that would supersede state-specific rules — a move the industry has long sought.

### 6. Shield AI Acquires Software Simulation Company

Defense AI company Shield AI announced an acquisition of a software simulation firm, continuing the trend of AI companies expanding into military and defense applications.

### 7. CrowdStrike + IBM Expand Cybersecurity Partnership

CrowdStrike and IBM announced deeper integration of CrowdStrike's AI-powered threat detection into IBM's security services. CrowdStrike noted attacks targeting public-facing applications are **up 44% year-over-year**.
