### Top Stories

#### 1. Markets Explode Higher on Iran Peace Hopes — Best Day in Weeks
Wall Street posted its best session in weeks as **all three major indices surged** on reports that Trump is willing to end the Iran war without requiring a full reopening of the Strait of Hormuz. The Nasdaq rocketed 3.83%, the S&P gained 2.91%, and the Dow climbed 2.49%.

Trump told allies to "get your own oil" and said the war **could end in 2-3 weeks**. Pakistani mediation efforts are underway. However, conflicting signals remain — Trump also threatened to destroy Iranian energy infrastructure if a deal isn't reached soon.

**Why it matters**: This was a pure relief rally. The Iran war has been the dominant force crushing markets for a month. Any credible path to de-escalation sends risk assets soaring and oil lower. But traders remain cautious — we've seen head-fakes before.

#### 2. Gas Hits $4 Per Gallon — Highest Since 2022
The average US gas price reached **$4.02 per gallon** on Tuesday, breaking through $4 for the first time since the aftermath of Russia's Ukraine invasion in 2022. California hit $5.91. Gas prices have surged 35% in just one month since the Iran war started Feb. 28.

The ripple effects are spreading: every 10% rise in fuel prices could push food costs up 2-3% due to higher fertilizer and transportation costs. Delivery and ride-share companies are increasing gas rebates for drivers. Summer travel plans are being reconsidered.

The Trump admin is fighting back: releasing 172M barrels from strategic reserves, removing sanctions on Russian oil, and the EPA loosened summertime gasoline blend regulations.

#### 3. McCormick Buys Unilever's Food Business for $45 Billion
Spice giant **McCormick** (owner of Old Bay, French's, Frank's RedHot) acquired Unilever's entire food division in a **~$45 billion** deal. About 70% of Unilever's food sales come from Hellmann's mayo and Knorr soup, which will join McCormick's portfolio pending regulatory approval.

This is separate from the $60B combined entity rumored last week — McCormick went straight for a full acquisition. Unilever is shedding food to focus on beauty and personal care amid changing consumer habits.

### Concept of the Day: Relief Rally

A **relief rally** is a sharp, sudden rise in stock prices that happens when a looming threat appears to diminish — even if nothing is definitively resolved yet.

Yesterday's market surge is a textbook example. For weeks, the Iran war crushed stocks through higher oil prices and inflation fears. When Trump signaled willingness to end the conflict, markets shot up almost 3-4% in a single day. The underlying problems (gas at $4, cooling jobs market, uncertain Fed) haven't changed — but the perceived probability of the worst-case scenario dropped.

The trap with relief rallies: they can be real turning points *or* they can be head-fakes. If the Iran situation deteriorates again, the rally reverses. The key question is whether peace talks produce concrete results in the "2-3 weeks" Trump mentioned.

Smart investors don't chase relief rallies. They watch whether the fundamentals actually improve in the days that follow.
