### Tech News

#### Claude Code Source Leak Reveals Hidden Features
The biggest story on Hacker News (1,200+ points): A deep dive into **leaked Claude Code source** reveals "fake tools" (tools that appear available but redirect behavior), "frustration regexes" (patterns that detect when users are getting frustrated and adjust responses), and an "undercover mode." The analysis provides a rare look at how AI coding assistants manage user interactions behind the scenes.

#### 1-Bit LLMs Go Commercial
**PrismML launched "1-Bit Bonsai"**, claiming the first commercially viable 1-bit large language models. These extremely compressed models use just 1 bit per parameter (instead of the typical 16 or 32 bits), dramatically reducing compute requirements while maintaining useful performance. This could democratize AI inference on edge devices.

#### TinyLoRA: Reasoning in 13 Parameters
A new paper, **"TinyLoRA — Learning to Reason in 13 Parameters"**, demonstrates that remarkably small model adaptations can enable reasoning capabilities. This challenges the assumption that bigger models are always needed for complex tasks.

#### Nvidia Invests $2B in Marvell Technology AI Partnership
**Nvidia poured $2 billion into Marvell Technology**, deepening their AI infrastructure partnership. Marvell designs custom AI chips for hyperscalers, and this investment signals Nvidia's strategy of building an ecosystem beyond its own GPUs.

#### Apple Turns 50
Apple celebrates its **50th anniversary** today — founded April 1, 1976. From a garage startup to the world's most valuable company ($3.7T market cap), with 2.5 billion active devices globally. The question now: can Apple remain dominant in an AI age where it's perceived as playing catch-up?

#### Oracle Lays Off Thousands
**Oracle began major layoffs** as it redirects billions toward AI data center buildouts. The company is making a massive bet on cloud AI infrastructure, but the human cost is significant.

### World News

#### Iran War: "Could End in 2-3 Weeks" — Trump
In the biggest geopolitical development of the week, **Trump told allies the Iran war could end in 2-3 weeks** while also telling them to "get your own oil." The mixed messaging continues — Trump is simultaneously signaling peace talks through Pakistani mediation and threatening to destroy Iranian energy infrastructure.

Oil fell below $100 briefly on the news before rebounding. World markets soared on the hope that the month-long conflict could wind down. The US Navy, however, faces practical challenges in "opening" the Strait of Hormuz, with analysts at Responsible Statecraft explaining why a military solution to the strait blockade is far more complex than Trump suggests.

#### Trump Approval Rating Hits New Low
According to The Economist's tracker, Trump's **net approval rating fell to -20** — eight percentage points below where he was at this stage in his first term, and the lowest of his second term. The Iran war and its economic fallout (gas prices, market turmoil) are the likely drivers.

#### SCOTUS Rules Against Conversion Therapy Ban
In an **8-1 decision**, the Supreme Court sided with a Christian counselor challenging Colorado's ban on conversion therapy for LGBTQ+ youth, citing First Amendment concerns. The case was sent back to a lower court where the ban is expected to be nullified. Over 20 states have similar laws that could now be challenged.

#### NASA Artemis Moon Mission Launches Today
Weather permitting, **NASA is sending four astronauts to orbit the moon** today — the first humans to leave low Earth orbit since 1972. The crew includes the first woman, person of color, and non-US citizen to make this journey. The 10-day trip is a key step in the $100 billion Artemis program to eventually land humans on the lunar surface again by 2028.

#### Venezuela's "Surreal Contrasts" Under Trump
The Economist reports from Caracas on the strange situation in the Venezuela that Trump claims to "run" — a land of contradictions where opposition hopes are stirring but the regime's grip remains uneven.
