President Trump released his FY2027 budget proposal requesting $1.5 trillion for defense — a **42% increase** over the current Pentagon allotment and the biggest single-year jump since World War II. Key items include:
- **5-7% military pay raise** for servicemembers

## Concept of the Day: WTI vs. Brent — Why Two Oil Prices?

You'll often see two crude oil prices quoted: **WTI** (West Texas Intermediate) and **Brent**. Here's the difference:

- **WTI** is the US benchmark, based on oil delivered to Cushing, Oklahoma. It's lighter and sweeter (less sulfur).
- **Brent** is the global benchmark, based on North Sea oil. It prices about 60% of the world's crude.

**Usually, Brent trades higher** than WTI because it reflects global supply/demand, while WTI is more US-focused.

**But right now, WTI ($111.54) is trading ABOVE Brent ($109.03)** — an unusual "inversion." This happened because Trump's Iran speech specifically threatened more US military action, making markets price in US-specific supply disruption risks (shipping through Hormuz, potential attacks on infrastructure). When the US is both the aggressor in a war AND a major oil consumer, domestic prices spike harder.

**Why it matters for your wallet:** Higher oil = higher gas prices this summer. Airlines that hedged their fuel costs early are sitting pretty; those that didn't are scrambling. This is why "jet fuel hedging" (which we covered yesterday) is suddenly the hottest topic in the airline industry.
