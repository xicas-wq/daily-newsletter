## Concept of the Day: IPO Valuation vs. Market Cap

When SpaceX says it wants a "$1.75 trillion valuation" at IPO, what does that mean?

**Valuation at IPO** = the IPO share price × total shares outstanding (including shares NOT being sold). It's a theoretical number representing what the entire company would be worth if every share traded at the IPO price.

**Market Cap** = current share price × shares outstanding. After the IPO, this fluctuates every second as the stock trades.

**Key insight:** SpaceX is only selling a fraction of its shares. If it raises $75B at a $1.75T valuation, that means it's selling roughly 4.3% of the company. The remaining ~95.7% stays with existing shareholders (like Musk). The actual market cap after trading begins could be higher or lower depending on demand.

**Connection to today:** If SpaceX's IPO succeeds at this valuation, Musk's ~42% stake would be worth ~$735B, potentially making him the world's first trillionaire when combined with his Tesla and other holdings.
