## Concept of the Day: Jet Fuel Hedging

**What is it?** Airlines buy fuel contracts months or years in advance at fixed prices to protect against sudden price spikes. This is called "hedging."

**Why it matters today:** The airlines that hedged their fuel costs before the Iran war are in much better shape than those that didn't. For example:
- **Southwest Airlines** is famously known for aggressive fuel hedging — it saved billions during past oil shocks
- **Carnival Cruise Lines** does NOT hedge fuel at all — analysts worry it could take a massive profit hit this year
- **Delta** saw fuel costs balloon by $400M in just one month

**Think of it like this:** Hedging is like locking in your rent price for 2 years. If rents skyrocket, you're protected. If they fall, you overpaid — but you got peace of mind. Airlines make the same bet with fuel.

**Key takeaway:** In volatile markets, hedging separates the companies that survive crises from those that get crushed by them. When you're evaluating airline or shipping stocks, always check their hedging strategy.
