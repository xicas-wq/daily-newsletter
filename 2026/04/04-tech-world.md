## Tech News

### Artemis II: Now Closer to the Moon Than Earth
NASA's Artemis II mission, launched April 1, has left Earth's orbit and is now **closer to the moon than Earth**. The four astronauts aboard the Orion capsule are on track for a lunar flyby on **Monday, April 6** — the first humans to reach the moon since 1972. The crew sent back stunning photos of Earth that went viral. The mission runs through April 11. Fun fact: This is the first spacecraft launched with a toilet — previous Apollo astronauts had to use bags. The $23 million space toilet was briefly offline at launch but is now fully operational (and extremely loud).

### Apple Turns 50 — "Blew a 5-Year Lead" on AI
CNBC reports that Apple celebrates its 50th anniversary this weekend. Former insiders say the iPhone maker "blew a 5-year lead" on AI but can still win. The company has been notably behind rivals like Google, OpenAI, and Meta in deploying consumer AI features, despite having early advantages in on-device processing and its massive installed base.

### OpenAI Buys Tech Podcast TBPN
OpenAI purchased the popular tech podcast TBPN (The Big Picture News) for an undisclosed amount, despite recently vowing to ditch "side quests." The move is seen as an attempt to control the narrative around AI, though the show says it will remain editorially independent. Meanwhile, OpenAI exec Fidji Simo (CEO of AGI Deployment) has taken medical leave as the company prepares for its IPO. COO Brad Lightcap is shifting to lead special projects.

### Google Workers' Activism Hits New Era
Bloomberg reports that Google employees protesting AI use for military applications are finding it's a "different era for activism." Workforce reductions at tech companies jumped **24% in March** year-over-year, leading US industries in announced cutbacks — making employees more cautious about speaking out.

### Tech Layoffs Continue
Tech sector job cuts led all US industries in March 2026, with a 24% YoY increase in announced reductions. Oracle's recent layoffs were among the most notable. The trend reflects both economic uncertainty from the Iran war and ongoing AI-driven restructuring.

### EFF Warns Against Weaponizing AI Procurement
The Electronic Frontier Foundation and other tech nonprofits warned the federal government not to "weaponize procurement to undermine AI trust and safety" — pushing back against efforts to strip safety requirements from AI tools purchased by government agencies.

## World News

### Iran War — Week 6: Two US Planes Downed, Missing Airman
The Iran war enters its 6th week with a dramatic escalation:
- **F-15E shot down** over Iran on Friday — pilot rescued, weapons systems officer still missing
- **A-10 Thunderbolt** also shot down — pilot safe
- Iran offering rewards for capturing the missing American alive
- Iran claims a US strike near a **nuclear plant** killed one person
- Another ship passed through the **Strait of Hormuz** on Saturday as Iran's toll system continues
- At least **15 US troops** have died since the war began, according to The Intercept
- Israel is separately **bombarding Beirut**, adding to regional chaos

The Guardian reports both US and Iranian forces are racing to find the missing pilot — a scenario that could trigger further escalation if Iran captures the airman alive.

### Trump's $2.2 Trillion Budget: Guns Over Butter
Trump's FY2027 budget proposal crystallizes wartime priorities:
- $1.5T for defense (+42%) — largest request in modern history
- 10% cuts to domestic programs (~$73B)
- EPA cut 52%, Small Business Administration cut 67%
- $10B for DC beautification projects
- NASA cut 23% (ironic timing given Artemis II's success)
- "We're fighting wars. We can't take care of daycare," Trump said

### Taiwan Secures LNG Supply Assurances
Taiwan announced it has received supply assurances from a "major" LNG-producing country, addressing concerns about energy security as the Iran conflict disrupts global supply chains. The unnamed country's commitment highlights how the Strait of Hormuz crisis is reshaping global energy alliances.

### India Buying Iranian Crude
India's Oil Ministry confirmed it is purchasing Iranian crude oil, rejecting claims of payment-led tanker diversions. The move positions India as a key beneficiary of discounted Iranian oil while Western nations maintain sanctions.

### Cuba to Release 2,000+ Political Prisoners
Cuba announced it would release more than 2,000 political prisoners as a humanitarian gesture, as the US continues to pressure the island nation.

### Italy's Soccer Coach Resigns
Italy's men's soccer head coach Gennaro Gattuso resigned after the country failed to qualify for its **third consecutive World Cup** — an unprecedented drought for the four-time champions.

### Westerners Fleeing in Record Numbers
The Economist compiled new data showing Western emigration has surged since the end of the COVID pandemic. Americans, Brits, and New Zealanders are leaving in notably higher numbers. Interestingly, the data suggests this may be economically beneficial rather than harmful. New Zealand is seeing the sharpest outflows, with many heading to Australia.
